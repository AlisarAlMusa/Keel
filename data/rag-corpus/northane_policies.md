# Northane University — Registration & Advising Policies (RAG corpus)

> One chunk per `## ` heading. DB-backed numbers (the 18-credit cap) must match
> the seed; advisory numbers here (withdrawal week, probation GPA, repeat/waitlist
> limits) are RAG-only guidance the engine does not enforce — they are
> deliberately different from Summit College so cross-tenant isolation is testable.

## Maximum Credits and Overload
A standard full-time load at Northane is 12–15 credits per semester. The hard cap
is 18 credits (enforced by the engine via each student's limit). A load of 16–18
credits is an overload and requires academic-advisor approval, granted to students
in good standing with a cumulative GPA at or above 3.0 and no active holds.
Overload requests are submitted before the registration window closes.

## Course Withdrawal
Students may withdraw from a course until the end of week 10. A withdrawal records
a "W" that does not affect GPA but counts in attempted-course history and may
affect progress-rate and financial aid. Withdrawing from a prerequisite course
delays any dependent course in the plan.

## Repeat-Course Policy
A passed course may not be retaken for credit. A failed course may be repeated;
the most recent grade is used for GPA while both attempts remain on the record.
Repeated failures of the same course trigger an advising review. The engine blocks
planning a course already passed.

## Prerequisite-Override Petition
A student who does not meet a course's prerequisites may petition for an exception,
stating their justification (relevant experience, equivalent coursework, or
instructor support). The petition is reviewed by the registrar and is a request to
a human — the engine still refuses to auto-enroll without the prerequisite.
Approval is not guaranteed.

## Graduation Application
Students apply for graduation in their final term once a degree audit confirms all
requirements are met or in progress. The registrar reviews; applying does not waive
any outstanding requirement, and an audit still showing missing requirements is not
approved.

## Academic Standing and Probation
Good standing requires a cumulative GPA at or above 2.0. A cumulative GPA below 2.0
places a student on academic probation, which may cap the credit load and requires
an advising plan. Two consecutive probation terms trigger a formal review.

## Waitlist Policy
When a section is full, students may join its waitlist as a first-class action and
are notified automatically when a seat opens. A student may hold up to three
waitlist positions at once. A waitlist seat is not guaranteed and does not satisfy
a prerequisite for any dependent course until enrollment is confirmed.

## Program / Major Change
A student in good standing (cumulative GPA at or above 2.0) may request one major
change per academic year. Credits already earned that satisfy the destination
program's requirements transfer automatically; the registrar reviews the request
and the recomputed degree audit before approval. A major change is a human-reviewed
request, not an automatic switch — the engine recomputes the audit and the new
timeline but never moves the student on its own.
