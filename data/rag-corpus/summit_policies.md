# Summit College — Registration & Advising Policies (RAG corpus)

> Second tenant. DB-backed numbers (the 18-credit cap) match the seed; advisory
> numbers (withdrawal week, probation GPA, repeat/waitlist limits, overload GPA)
> are deliberately DIFFERENT from Northane so a cross-tenant leak is visible — a
> Summit policy query must return Summit's numbers, never Northane's.

## Maximum Credits and Overload
A standard full-time load at Summit is 12–15 credits per semester. The hard cap is
18 credits (engine-enforced). A load of 16–18 credits is an overload requiring
program-director approval, available to students with a cumulative GPA at or above
3.25 and no holds. Overload requests close one week before registration opens.

## Course Withdrawal
Students may withdraw until the end of week 9. A withdrawal records a "W" that does
not affect GPA but counts in attempted-course history and progress-rate.
Withdrawing from a prerequisite course delays any dependent course in the plan.

## Repeat-Course Policy
A passed course may not be retaken for credit. A failed course may be repeated up
to two times; the highest grade counts toward GPA while all attempts remain on the
record. A third failure requires a director meeting before re-enrollment. The
engine blocks planning a course already passed.

## Prerequisite-Override Petition
Students who do not meet prerequisites may petition the registrar with a
justification. The petition is a human-reviewed request, not an automatic bypass —
the engine still refuses to auto-enroll without the prerequisite. Summit
additionally requires a short statement of readiness for quantitative courses.

## Graduation Application
Graduation is applied for in the final term after a degree audit confirms all
requirements are met or in progress. The registrar reviews; an audit with missing
requirements is not approved. Summit requires the capstone (CS420) to be in
progress or complete at application time.

## Academic Standing and Probation
Good standing requires a cumulative GPA at or above 2.25 (higher than some peer
institutions). Below 2.25 places a student on probation with a capped load and a
required advising plan. Two consecutive probation terms trigger review.

## Waitlist Policy
A full section offers a waitlist as a first-class action with automatic seat-open
notification. Summit limits a student to two concurrent waitlist positions. A
waitlist seat is not guaranteed and does not satisfy a prerequisite until
enrollment is confirmed.

## Program / Major Change
Summit requires a cumulative GPA at or above 2.5 and a meeting with the destination
program's director before a major change is approved. For full credit transfer the
change must be requested before the start of the junior year; later requests may
forfeit elective credit. The request is human-reviewed — the engine recomputes the
audit and the new timeline but never switches the student automatically.
